STA 553 DATA VISUALIZATION

Ethics in Data Visualization
1. Designer’s Perspective
  • Truthful Encoding: axis starts at zero, area scales by value.
  • Accessibility baseline: colorblind-safe, ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) labels, contrast.
  • Clarity over chartjunk: avoid 3D distortion, misleading animation.
  • Annotation mandate: surface uncertainty & missing data.
2. User’s Perspective
  • Pre-attentive exploitation: red for trivial data manipulates.
  • Baseline framing: truncated axis triggers panic or calm.
  • Cognitive overload forces blind trust in designer.
  • Realism uncanny valley: 3D maps suppress skepticism.
3. Data Journalism
  • Single insight tyranny: omit trend = lie by omission.
  • Emotionalization: icons override quantitative reality.
  • Source equivalence: unverified data demands caveat.
  • Iterative correction: transparent version history.
4. Algorithmic / ML
  • Auto‑bin slicing creates false clusters.
  • Benchmark corruption: traffic lights mislead.
  • Proxy variable visualization sanitizes bias.
  • Temporal blindness ignores disaster context.
5. Persuasion & Marketing
  • Truncated do-gooder: false emergency for donations.
  • Confusion as conversion: hidden lifetime cost.
  • Vanity metrics distract from churn rate.
  • Comparative anchoring without absolute context.
6. DEI Perspective
  • God trick: default viewpoint hides power structures.
  • Binarization erases non‑binary identities.
  • Color scales reinforce harmful associations.
  • Deficit‑based framing strips agency.
7. Open-Source & Tooling
  • Defaults encode bias (Mercator, red‑green scales).
  • Vendor lock‑in holds narratives hostage.
  • Granular hover exposes personal microdata.
  • Exploitation of open source for surveillance.
8. Environmental & Futures
  • Carbon cost of real‑time dashboards.
  • Normalizing apocalypse: climate doomism.
  • Self‑fulfilling prophecy visualizations.
  • E‑waste from GPU‑heavy 3D rendering.
