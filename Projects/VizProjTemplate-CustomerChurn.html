Telco Customer Churn Dataset · IBM Sample · Q1 2020 – Q3 2023 | Data Storytelling Report

1. Motivation & Core Questions

Background: The company experiences 2–3% monthly churn (≈27% annualized). Dataset includes 7,043 residential postpaid customers, 21 features. Key drivers: contract type, tenure, monthly charges, and add-on services.

📅 Tenure: 0–72 months 💸 Monthly charges $18–$119 ⚠️ Churn rate: ~27%

✅ Business Questions (Click to track your review)

📌 Hypotheses: H1: Month-to-month contract customers churn at significantly higher rates. H2: Fiber optic customers without security/tech support add-ons churn more than DSL. H3: Customers with tenure under 12 months represent the largest share of churn volume.

2. Exploratory Analysis & Data Quality

Shape: 7,043 rows × 21 columns. Missing TotalCharges (11 rows, tenure=0) → imputed 0. No behavioral logs, but tenure + contract + monthly charges provide strong signals.

📈 Univariate snapshot: tenure (right-skewed) | MonthlyCharges multimodal (pricing tiers) | Churn base rate ≈27% | SeniorCitizen ~16%

📌 Visual 3: High-Risk Cluster (Monthly Charges vs Tenure)

Scatter plot with marginal histograms reveals: dense cluster of churned customers (red) in top-left quadrant: tenure <12 months & MonthlyCharges > $70 — high-value Fiber optic month-to-month.

🧩 High risk zone: tenure <12mo and charges >$70 → churn rate ≈68% in that segment. Revenue loss: high ARPU customers leaving fast.
🔍 Marginal notes: monthly charges trimodal (pricing tiers: low ~$30, medium ~$65, high ~$90+). Retained customers fanned after 20 months.
💡 Business implication: This segment generates negative lifetime value. Bundle security & tech support, offer contract conversion incentives.

🎯 4. Narrative & Business Decisions

Setup: $12.3M annualized revenue at risk. Conflict: Month-to-month churn near 50%, early tenure and Fiber without sticky add-ons. Resolution: Sticky services and annual contracts drive loyalty. Action: retention campaigns at month 3, free security bundle for fiber, annual contract discount.

✅ Recommended Actions (tracking)

Expected impact: 15% reduction in month-to-month churn → $2.1M saved annually; Fiber high-ARPU retention lift → $800K.

⚠️ Caveats & future modeling

Single snapshot, no causal inference. Use tree-based models (XGBoost/Random Forest) and derive num_addon_services stickiness score. Also test interaction: MonthlyCharges × Contract.

📋 Dashboard Design & Monitoring

Live dashboard KPIs: Churn rate (with MoM arrow), stacked bar by contract, revenue-at-risk heatmap, SHAP top drivers. Refreshed daily, model weekly retraining. Alerts if churn >30% or high-risk segment grows >10% MoM.
