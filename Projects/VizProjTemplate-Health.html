📌 1. Motivation & Hypotheses

Dataset: 4,500 adults (40–70y, diabetes-free at baseline), 9-year follow-up. Incident T2D confirmed via glucose ≥126 mg/dL, HbA1c ≥6.5%, or physician dx.

🔍 Core Questions
  • 9-year cumulative incidence by glycemic status & race/ethnicity?
  • Does hs-CRP mediate obesity→diabetes?
  • Does neighborhood SES modify lifestyle protection?
🧪 Hypotheses
  • H1: Healthy lifestyle → ≥25% lower hazard of T2D, BMI-independent.
  • H2: hs-CRP mediates ≥15% of waist–diabetes association.
  • H3: Lifestyle–diabetes association weaker in low-SES (interaction p<0.05).

📈 Visual 1: Cumulative Incidence by Glycemic Status × Race/Ethnicity

🔬 9-year incidence: 42% (prediabetes) vs 8% (normoglycemia). Among prediabetes: Black 48% vs White 35%.
📉 Curves diverge early → screening at age 40 justified, disparities demand targeted prevention.

🩸 Visual 2: Mediation by hs-CRP (Obesity → Inflammation → Diabetes)

📊 Total effect HR = 1.64 · Indirect (via CRP) HR = 1.14 · Proportion mediated = 28% (95% CI: 18–38%)

Inflammation explains ~one-quarter of obesity–diabetes pathway, supporting hs-CRP as risk stratification marker.

🏙️ Visual 3: Lifestyle × Neighborhood SES Interaction

🌿 High nSES: healthy lifestyle → 5% risk vs 18% (HR=0.52). Low nSES: 12% vs 19% (HR=0.78). Interaction p = 0.008 → structural context constrains individual benefits.

💡 Implication: Pair lifestyle programs with neighborhood investment (food access, safe recreation).

📐 Exploration Plan & Data Quality

Missing data: MICE (25 imputations) primary; sensitivity: complete-case, pattern-mixture models. Cumulative incidence (1-KM), DAG for mediation pathway. Cox models with interaction term for nSES × lifestyle.

  • Univariate: Baseline Table 1 stratified, exposure distributions (log-transform CRP).
  • Bivariate/Multivariate: correlation heatmap, KM curves by BMI, lifestyle tertiles.
⚠️ Caveats: Observational data – causal claims limited. Healthy volunteer bias; 12% loss to follow-up (IPCW planned). Replication needed for neighborhood interaction.

⚡ Narrative & Actionable Insights

🎯 Clinical Actions

  • Universal prediabetes screening at age 40+.
  • Lifestyle programs for screen-positives + hs-CRP risk stratification.
  • Consider inflammation as intervention target.

🏛️ Public Health & Research

  • Neighborhood investment: food access, safe recreation zones.
  • Replicate neighborhood interaction findings before resource allocation.
  • Test anti-inflammatory strategies in high-CRP cohorts.

🧾 Key Cohort Characteristics & Limitations

Key variables: Demographics, family history, BMI, waist circumference, physical activity, AHEI diet quality, smoking, fasting glucose, HbA1c, hs-CRP, neighborhood SES index, incident T2D, time-to-event.

Limitations: Healthy volunteer bias, 12% loss to follow-up (IPCW planned), self-reported diet/activity non-differential misclassification, residual confounding. PAF estimates assume causality; interaction requires replication.
